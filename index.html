My Quarantine Fried Rice Recipe ¯\_(ツ)_/¯

A simple, step-by-step guide for a delicious meal I learned to make during quarantine.

by Jack Gil

Some Context

Before the start of this whole shutdown, I had been living in a college dorm room with two other people, in a unit with no real kitchen space to use and make my own meals. I relied mostly on the meal plan provided by the university throughout my first semester and the first half of my second semester. Since I didn't have normal access to a kitchen, I wasn't really able to cook for myself at all.

But when the covid-19 pandemic shutdown happened, all of a sudden everyone started preparing to leave town! Soon after, I moved into my current residence at my fraternity's house, where we have a large shared kitchen space. I realized, despite the turmoil the shutdown had put me through, one small silver lining was that I finally had the opportunity to really learn to experiment with cooking in ways that I hadn't been able to before.

Neither my partner Chris nor myself were particularly good at cooking anything beyond scrambled eggs at first, but that didn't stop us from trying new things. Using the leftover rice and canned veggies my partner's housemates had left in their kitchen after they'd moved back home, we made our first attempt at fried rice.

I asked my partner what he thought of this dish that had become a staple of our cooking these past few months in lockdown.

"I had this huge bag of rice," Chris said, thinking back to mid-March. "We were starving," he exaggerated dramatically, "and we didn't want to order out as much because we wanted to save money and produce less trash."

The following recipe we came up with quickly became one of our go-to dinner meals, especially because of how easy it is to prepare and alter to suit our changing cravings. So if you've been hungry for a simple, low-stress recipe, this is perfect for you.


Ingredients

    - White or brown rice.
    - Vegetable oil.
    - Canned or chopped vegetables of your choosing (corn, chickpeas, etc).
    - At least half an onion.
    - Spices of your choosing. We use a combo of turmeric, cumin powder, garlic powder, and cayenne pepper.
    - At least two eggs.
